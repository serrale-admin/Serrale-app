## 3. Information we collect

### 3.1 Information you provide as a customer

When you browse, request help, contact providers, or use customer features, we may collect:

- name;
- phone number;
- WhatsApp number, if you provide it;
- service need or request description;
- selected service category;
- preferred area, city, neighborhood, or location details;
- request date, time, status, and related notes;
- OTP verification information;
- account/session information if customer login is enabled;
- messages, feedback, complaints, safety reports, or support requests you send to SERRALE;
- images, screenshots, or attachments you voluntarily submit to support.

You may browse many parts of SERRALE as a guest. Guest browsing may include viewing categories, opening provider profiles, searching, calling providers, or opening WhatsApp links. Some actions, such as submitting a request or using account-based features, may require phone verification.

### 3.2 Information you provide as a provider

When you register, join, publish, update, or verify a provider listing, we may collect:

- full name;
- phone number;
- WhatsApp number;
- service category;
- service area, city, neighborhood, or operating location;
- experience level;
- biography or service description;
- provider profile image;
- service photos, work samples, or portfolio items if provided;
- availability, price range, service radius, or other listing details if supported;
- national ID number, national ID image, business license, certificate, or other verification material if requested for provider trust, safety, onboarding, or compliance;
- provider account/session information;
- support, moderation, review, complaint, or safety communications.

Provider listings are designed to be public. Information such as provider name, category, area, experience, bio, public profile image, phone number, and WhatsApp link may be displayed to users so they can evaluate and contact providers.

Private verification information, including private ID images or private verification documents, is not intended to be publicly displayed as part of a provider listing.

### 3.3 Phone number and OTP verification information

SERRALE uses phone OTP verification to confirm that a user controls a phone number.

We may collect or generate:

- submitted phone number;
- OTP challenge identifier;
- OTP purpose, such as customer request, provider join, provider login, or phone-change verification;
- OTP attempt count;
- OTP status, expiry, and verification timestamp;
- rate-limit and anti-abuse metadata;
- session tokens or session metadata after successful verification.

We do not ask you to share your OTP code with SERRALE staff. You should never give your OTP code to anyone who contacts you outside the official app, website, or verified support process.

### 3.4 Profile images, service photos, and uploaded files

If a provider uploads a profile image, service photo, work example, ID image, certificate, screenshot, or other file, we may collect:

- the uploaded file;
- file name or generated storage path;
- file type, size, and upload time;
- related provider or support record;
- moderation, review, or verification status if applicable.

Public listing images may be visible to users. Private verification files are treated as restricted information and are used for verification, trust, safety, support, dispute handling, or legal compliance where applicable.

You must not upload images or files that you do not have the right to use, that contain another person's private information without permission, or that are illegal, misleading, abusive, exploitative, obscene, hateful, or unsafe.

### 3.5 Location and area information

SERRALE may collect location-related information in different ways:

- area, city, neighborhood, or service location typed by a user;
- provider operating area or service radius;
- approximate location selected by a provider for nearby matching;
- device location only if the app or website asks for permission and you allow it;
- location derived from IP address or device/network information for security, fraud prevention, abuse prevention, or analytics.

Where nearby matching is supported, SERRALE may use location to show relevant providers or distance-based results. We aim to avoid exposing raw private coordinates publicly. Public results may show approximate distance or area rather than precise private coordinates.

You can control device-level location permission through your phone settings.

### 3.6 Contact actions, calls, WhatsApp, and sharing

SERRALE may log events such as:

- provider profile view;
- call button click;
- WhatsApp button click;
- copy-phone action;
- share action;
- search result click;
- request-help submission.

These events help us understand demand, detect abuse, improve provider ranking and service quality, measure marketplace health, and support safety reviews. A call or WhatsApp click is a contact attempt; it does not prove that a call was answered, that a conversation happened, or that a job was completed.

When you tap call or WhatsApp, your phone, dialer, browser, or WhatsApp application may process the action. Those third-party services have their own privacy practices.

### 3.7 Search, browsing, and usage information

We may collect information about how SERRALE is used, such as:

- search terms;
- categories viewed;
- providers viewed;
- filters used;
- area or location parameters;
- app screens visited;
- buttons tapped;
- language preference;
- app version;
- session activity;
- feature usage;
- timestamps and request paths.

We use this information to improve search quality, identify demand gaps, improve categories, detect spam, and understand where users need better service coverage.

### 3.8 Device, technical, log, and diagnostic information

When you use SERRALE, we may automatically collect technical information, including:

- device type, operating system, app version, and browser type;
- IP address or approximate network information;
- language and region settings;
- crash reports, error logs, performance logs, and diagnostic information;
- request IDs, timestamps, API route names, response status, and latency;
- security and anti-abuse signals;
- source platform, such as mobile app, web, admin, Telegram bot, or support channel.

We use technical information to operate the service, investigate errors, maintain security, prevent abuse, improve performance, and meet platform requirements.

### 3.9 Payment, contract, escrow, and professional-marketplace information

SERRALE Basic mobile does not currently require in-app payments, escrow, contracts, or SERRALE Plus marketplace authentication. However, if SERRALE offers legacy or future marketplace features involving projects, proposals, contracts, payments, escrow, subscriptions, verification fees, promoted listings, or business accounts, we may collect information necessary to operate those features.

This may include:

- business name and contact details;
- project, proposal, contract, invoice, or payment metadata;
- transaction references;
- payment provider response data;
- dispute, refund, compliance, and support records.

Where SERRALE uses third-party payment processors, those processors may collect and process payment information under their own terms and privacy policies. SERRALE should not store full card numbers unless a compliant payment processor or legal requirement supports it.
